# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

The Ihsan Agile community is grounded in Islamic values of dignity (*karāmah*), justice (*'adl*), and sincere advice (*naṣīḥah*). We are committed to making participation in this project a respectful, enriching experience for everyone.

## Our Values in Practice

**Adab (Proper Conduct)**: We engage with courtesy, patience, and professionalism in all interactions.

**Ḥusn al-Ẓann (Good Faith)**: We assume good intentions and give others the benefit of the doubt.

**Shūrā (Consultation)**: We value diverse perspectives and engage in collaborative decision-making.

**Ikhlāṣ (Sincerity)**: We contribute with sincere intention to serve the ummah and advance the framework's mission.

**Respect for Knowledge**: We honor both Islamic scholarship and Agile expertise, recognizing contributors bring different forms of valuable knowledge.

## Expected Behavior

- Treat all participants with respect and dignity
- Engage constructively, even in disagreement
- Welcome newcomers and support their learning
- Respect diverse schools of Islamic thought
- Stay focused on the framework's mission
- Give credit to others' ideas and contributions

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Disrespect toward Islamic sources or sincere practitioners
- Personal attacks, harassment, or intimidation
- Sectarian arguments or takfīr
- Discrimination based on background, identity, or affiliation
- Disrupting productive discussions
- Plagiarism or failing to credit others' work

## Scope

This code applies to:
- GitHub repository interactions (Issues, PRs, Discussions)
- Pilot program participation
- Workshops and community events
- All Ihsan Agile community spaces

## Enforcement

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior:
- **Contact**: getinvolved@ihsanagile.org
- **Response time**: Within 48 hours
- **Process**: Review, investigation, appropriate action
- **Possible actions**: Warning, temporary suspension, permanent ban

We are committed to fair, transparent enforcement guided by Islamic principles of justice.

## Attribution

This code of conduct is inspired by Islamic ethics and informed by the Contributor Covenant v2.1.
